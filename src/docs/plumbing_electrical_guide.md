# Plumbing and Electrical Maintenance Guide
## Standard Operating Procedures for Facilities Maintenance

---

## PART 1: PLUMBING SYSTEMS

### 1. Domestic Water System Maintenance

#### Monthly Tasks
- Check water pressure at key locations (target: 40-80 PSI)
- Inspect for visible leaks at fixtures, valves, and connections
- Test backflow prevention devices
- Check water heater temperature settings (120 degrees F recommended)
- Inspect sump pumps and test float switches

#### Quarterly Tasks
- Flush water heater to remove sediment buildup
- Test pressure relief valves on water heaters
- Inspect expansion tanks for proper charge
- Check water treatment systems (softeners, filters)
- Inspect and clean aerators on faucets
- Test emergency eyewash stations and safety showers

#### Annual Tasks
- Professional backflow preventer testing (required by code)
- Water heater anode rod inspection (replace if depleted)
- Full system pressure test
- Inspect and service water softening equipment
- Water quality testing (lead, bacteria, pH)
- Update plumbing as-built drawings if modifications were made

### 2. Drainage and Sewer System

#### Monthly Tasks
- Check floor drains for proper flow
- Inspect grease traps (food service areas)
- Verify sump pump operation
- Check for slow drains or gurgling (signs of blockage)

#### Quarterly Tasks
- Clean grease traps
- Camera inspect main sewer lines (annually for older buildings)
- Jet clean main drain lines in high-use areas
- Inspect roof drains and downspouts for blockage
- Check sewer clean-out access points

#### Annual Tasks
- Full sewer line camera inspection
- Root treatment if tree roots are present
- Inspect and test sewer ejector pumps
- Verify compliance with grease trap maintenance requirements

### 3. Hot Water System Maintenance

#### For Storage Tank Water Heaters
- Monthly: Check temperature and pressure relief valve
- Quarterly: Flush 5 gallons from drain valve to remove sediment
- Annually: Inspect anode rod (replace when less than 50% remaining)
- Every 5 years: Consider full tank flush and internal inspection
- **Typical lifespan: 8-12 years. Plan replacement at year 10.**

#### For Tankless Water Heaters
- Annually: Descale with vinegar solution
- Annually: Clean inlet water filter
- Every 2 years: Check heat exchanger for scale buildup

### 4. Plumbing Troubleshooting

#### Symptom: Low Water Pressure
1. Check PRV (pressure reducing valve) setting
2. Inspect for partially closed isolation valves
3. Check for water main issues (contact utility)
4. Inspect aerators and strainers for mineral buildup
5. Check water softener bypass valve position
6. Test pressure at water meter vs. point of use

#### Symptom: Water Heater Not Producing Hot Water
1. Check thermostat setting
2. Verify power supply (electric) or gas supply (gas)
3. Check pilot light or ignition system
4. Test heating elements for continuity (electric)
5. Check for sediment buildup (flush tank)
6. Inspect dip tube for deterioration

#### Symptom: Sewer Odor in Building
1. Check floor drain traps (dry traps allow sewer gas in)
2. Pour water into unused floor drains to refill traps
3. Inspect wax rings on toilets
4. Check vent pipe connections for disconnection
5. Inspect sewer line for breaks or blockages

## PART 2: ELECTRICAL SYSTEMS

### 1. Electrical Distribution Maintenance

#### Monthly Tasks
- Visual inspection of main electrical panels
- Check for signs of overheating (discoloration, burning smell)
- Verify all panel covers are in place and secured
- Test GFCI outlets in wet locations
- Check emergency lighting operation
- Verify exit signs are illuminated

#### Quarterly Tasks
- Thermal imaging scan of main distribution panels
- Check and tighten electrical connections in panels
- Test transfer switch operation (for generator backup)
- Inspect and test surge protection devices
- Check lighting control systems and timers

#### Annual Tasks
- Comprehensive electrical system study
- Megger testing on main feeders
- Arc flash hazard analysis review
- Test all circuit breakers (trip test)
- Inspect and test grounding system
- Update electrical one-line diagrams if changes were made

### 2. Emergency Generator Maintenance

#### Weekly Tasks
- Visual inspection (oil level, coolant level, fuel level)
- Check block heater operation
- Verify battery charger operation
- Check for fuel leaks or coolant leaks
- Review any fault codes on controller

#### Monthly Tasks
- Start and run under load for minimum 30 minutes
- Record all operating parameters (voltage, frequency, temperature)
- Check oil pressure and coolant temperature during run
- Test automatic transfer switch operation
- Inspect exhaust system for leaks

#### Quarterly Tasks
- Load bank test at 75% rated capacity minimum
- Change oil and oil filter
- Replace fuel filters
- Test battery and charging system
- Check coolant concentration and condition

#### Annual Tasks
- Full load bank test (2 hours minimum)
- Replace air filter
- Coolant system flush and refill
- Full electrical connection inspection
- Fuel tank inspection and treatment
- Update test log for regulatory compliance

### 3. UPS (Uninterruptible Power Supply) Maintenance

#### Monthly Tasks
- Check UPS status indicators and alarms
- Record input/output voltage and load percentage
- Verify bypass operation
- Check battery temperature

#### Quarterly Tasks
- Run UPS on battery (controlled discharge test)
- Clean air filters on UPS cabinet
- Check battery connections for corrosion
- Verify environmental conditions (temperature, humidity)

#### Annual Tasks
- Full battery impedance test
- Capacitor inspection
- Comprehensive electrical testing
- Replace batteries per manufacturer schedule (typically 3-5 years)

### 4. Electrical Troubleshooting

#### Symptom: Frequent Breaker Trips
1. Check for overloaded circuits (add up connected loads)
2. Inspect for short circuits (damaged wiring, moisture)
3. Test breaker for proper trip curve
4. Check for ground faults
5. Thermal scan connections for hot spots
6. **If breaker trips repeatedly with no clear cause, replace the breaker**

#### Symptom: Flickering Lights
1. Check bulb connections (tighten or replace)
2. Inspect switch and dimmer connections
3. Check for loose neutral connections at panel
4. Verify voltage at outlet (should be 118-122V)
5. Check for utility voltage fluctuations
6. Inspect for harmonic distortion from VFDs or electronic loads

#### Symptom: Emergency Generator Fails to Start
1. Check battery voltage (should be above 24V DC)
2. Verify fuel supply (check gauge AND physical level)
3. Check for fault codes on controller
4. Inspect coolant level and temperature
5. Check starter motor connections
6. Verify transfer switch signal is reaching generator
7. **Generator failure is a CRITICAL issue. Notify facilities supervisor immediately.**

### 5. Electrical Safety Requirements

#### Required Safety Equipment
- Arc flash PPE rated for incident energy level
- Voltage-rated gloves with leather protectors
- Safety glasses with side shields
- Non-conductive hard hat (Class E)
- Insulated tools rated for working voltage

#### Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) Procedures
1. Notify all affected personnel
2. Shut down equipment using normal procedure
3. Isolate energy sources (open disconnects, close valves)
4. Apply lockout devices and tags
5. Verify zero energy state with voltage tester
6. Test voltage tester on known source before and after
7. **NEVER bypass a lockout. NEVER remove another person's lock.**

#### Permits Required
- Hot work permit: Required for any work near energized conductors
- Confined space permit: Required for work in vaults or manholes
- Excavation permit: Required before digging near underground utilities
