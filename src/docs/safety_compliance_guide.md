# Safety and Compliance Guide
## Regulatory Requirements for Facilities Maintenance

### 1. Occupational Health and Safety

#### General Safety Requirements
- All maintenance work must comply with OSHA standards (29 CFR 1910/1926)
- Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) must be worn as appropriate for the task
- Safety Data Sheets (SDS) must be available for all chemicals used
- All incidents, near-misses, and injuries must be reported within 24 hours
- Regular safety meetings required (minimum monthly)

#### Hazard Communication
- All chemicals must be properly labeled
- SDS must be accessible to all workers
- Workers must be trained on chemical hazards they may encounter
- Chemical inventory must be updated annually
- Incompatible chemicals must be stored separately

#### Fall Protection
- Fall protection required at heights of 4 feet or more (general industry)
- Fall protection required at heights of 6 feet or more (construction)
- Roof work requires guardrails, safety nets, or personal fall arrest systems
- Ladders must be inspected before each use
- Three points of contact must be maintained when climbing

### 2. Fire Safety Compliance

#### Fire Suppression Systems
- Sprinkler systems must be inspected quarterly (visual) and annually (full test)
- Fire extinguishers inspected monthly, serviced annually
- Kitchen hood suppression systems inspected semi-annually
- Fire pump tested weekly (churn test) and annually (full flow test)
- All inspections must be documented and records retained for 3 years minimum

#### Fire Alarm Systems
- Monthly testing of fire alarm pull stations (rotating basis)
- Annual sensitivity testing of smoke detectors
- Battery backup must support 24 hours standby plus 5 minutes alarm
- Fire alarm monitoring service must be maintained 24/7
- Any impairment to fire protection must be reported to the fire department

#### Means of Egress
- Exit paths must remain clear at all times
- Exit signs must be illuminated and visible from required distance
- Emergency lighting must activate within 10 seconds of power loss
- Minimum 90 minutes of emergency lighting battery backup
- Fire doors must not be propped open (unless held by magnetic release)

### 3. Environmental Compliance

#### Refrigerant Management (EPA Section 608)
- Only EPA-certified technicians may handle refrigerants
- All refrigerant additions and recoveries must be logged
- Leak repair required within 30 days for systems over 50 lbs charge
- Record keeping required for minimum 3 years
- Refrigerant must be recovered before equipment disposal

#### Hazardous Waste
- Identify and classify all hazardous waste generated
- Store hazardous waste in approved containers with proper labels
- Maximum 90-day storage without permit (large quantity generators)
- Manifest required for all hazardous waste shipments
- Annual report to EPA/state agency required

#### Asbestos Management
- Buildings constructed before 1980 may contain asbestos materials
- Asbestos survey required before any renovation or demolition
- Only licensed abatement contractors may disturb asbestos
- Maintain asbestos management plan and building survey records
- Annual notification to building occupants if asbestos is present

#### Lead Paint
- Buildings constructed before 1978 may contain lead paint
- Lead assessment required before disturbing painted surfaces
- EPA RRP (Renovation, Repair, Painting) Rule applies
- Only certified firms may perform lead paint work
- Proper containment and waste disposal required

### 4. Elevator Compliance

#### Code Requirements (ASME A17.1 / CSA B44)
- Annual Category 1 test by certified inspector
- 5-year Category 5 full load test
- Maintain current inspection certificate posted in car
- Report accidents to authority having jurisdiction within 24 hours
- All modifications require plan review and permit
- Emergency communication must connect to 24/7 monitoring

#### Accessibility Requirements (ADA/AODA)
- Car controls at accessible height (48 inches max)
- Braille and raised characters on all hoistway and car buttons
- Audible floor announcements
- Door reopening device (safety edge or photo eye)
- Minimum door opening time per code

### 5. Boiler and Pressure Vessel Compliance

#### Inspection Requirements
- Annual inspection by authorized inspector
- Certificate of inspection must be posted
- Safety relief valves tested annually
- Low water cutoff tested regularly (weekly recommended)
- Operator must hold valid boiler operator license where required
- Maintain inspection records for life of the equipment

#### Operating Requirements
- Written operating procedures must be available
- Emergency shutdown procedures posted near equipment
- Water treatment program required and monitored
- Stack emissions must comply with local air quality regulations
- Annual combustion efficiency test recommended

### 6. Electrical Compliance

#### National Electrical Code (NEC/CEC)
- All electrical work must comply with current edition
- Permits required for electrical installations and modifications
- Licensed electrician required for all work
- Arc flash labels required on all panels and equipment
- Working clearances must be maintained per code (36 inches minimum)

#### Emergency Power (NFPA 110)
- Emergency generator must be tested monthly under load
- Annual 4-hour load test required for Level 1 systems
- Automatic transfer within 10 seconds for emergency systems
- Fuel supply for minimum 2 hours at full load
- Maintenance performed per manufacturer and NFPA 110 requirements

### 7. Indoor Air Quality

#### ASHRAE Standard 62.1 Ventilation Requirements
- Minimum outdoor air ventilation rates must be maintained
- Air filters must be replaced on schedule
- Cooling coils and drain pans must be kept clean
- CO2 monitoring recommended in densely occupied spaces
- Annual IAQ assessment recommended

#### Mold Prevention
- Maintain relative humidity below 60%
- Respond to water intrusion within 24-48 hours
- Inspect HVAC systems for moisture and microbial growth
- Clean and disinfect drain pans and humidifiers regularly
- Document remediation activities

### 8. Record Keeping Requirements

#### Minimum Retention Periods
- Elevator inspection records: 5 years
- Fire protection inspection records: 3 years
- Refrigerant management records: 3 years
- Asbestos records: 30 years (or life of building)
- Hazardous waste manifests: 3 years
- Boiler inspection records: life of equipment
- Electrical inspection records: 3 years
- Employee safety training records: duration of employment plus 3 years
- Work order history: 7 years recommended

#### Documentation Best Practices
- All maintenance work must be documented in the CMMS
- Include date, technician, work performed, parts used, and time spent
- Attach photos of conditions found and work completed
- Record any deferred maintenance items with justification
- Update asset condition ratings after each maintenance event
- Maintain chain of custody for compliance-critical records
