# Elevator Maintenance Guide
## Standard Operating Procedures for Facilities Maintenance

### 1. Passenger Elevator Preventive Maintenance

#### Monthly Tasks
- Ride each elevator, checking for unusual sounds or vibrations
- Test door operation (open/close speed, reversal on obstruction)
- Check car lighting and ventilation
- Test emergency phone/intercom
- Inspect car and hoistway door tracks for debris
- Check door gibs and guides for wear
- Verify floor leveling accuracy

#### Quarterly Tasks
- Lubricate guide rails and guide shoes
- Inspect and adjust door operator mechanism
- Check safety edges and photo eyes
- Test car top emergency stop switch
- Inspect governor and governor rope
- Check controller room ventilation and cleanliness
- Inspect pit for water intrusion and cleanliness
- Test pit stop switch

#### Semi-Annual Tasks
- Inspect wire ropes for wear, broken strands, and diameter reduction
- Check sheave grooves for wear
- Inspect brake operation and lining
- Test overspeed governor
- Check electrical connections in controller
- Inspect traveling cables for wear
- Measure door closing force (must not exceed 30 lbs per code)

#### Annual Tasks (Full Inspection)
- Category 1 test (ASME A17.1 required annually)
- Load test
- Full electrical inspection
- Inspect buffers and buffer switches
- Test all safety devices
- Check machine alignment
- Update inspection certificate
- File compliance report with local authority

### 2. Freight Elevator Specific Procedures

#### Additional Monthly Checks
- Inspect power-operated vertical lift gates
- Check platform condition for damage or warping
- Verify capacity signage is visible and accurate
- Inspect car gate interlock switches
- Check overhead clearance warning signs

#### Additional Annual Checks
- Structural inspection of car frame and platform
- Load test at 125% rated capacity
- Check guide rail alignment
- Inspect hydraulic system (if applicable):
  - Check hydraulic fluid level and condition
  - Inspect cylinder and packing for leaks
  - Test pressure relief valve
  - Check pump operation and noise level

### 3. Hydraulic Elevator Maintenance

#### Monthly Tasks
- Check hydraulic oil level (should be within sight glass range)
- Inspect for oil leaks at cylinder, fittings, and valves
- Check oil temperature (should not exceed 150 degrees F)
- Listen for pump cavitation or unusual noise
- Test manual lowering valve

#### Quarterly Tasks
- Take oil sample for analysis
- Check machine room ventilation
- Inspect oil lines and fittings
- Test pressure relief valve
- Check leveling accuracy at all floors

#### Annual Tasks
- Full oil analysis (viscosity, contamination, moisture)
- Replace hydraulic oil filter
- Inspect cylinder for corrosion (underground cylinders: PVC liner check)
- Test overspeed valve
- Inspect underground piping if applicable

### 4. Troubleshooting Common Elevator Issues

#### Symptom: Door Not Closing Properly
1. Check door track for debris or obstructions
2. Inspect door rollers and hangers for wear
3. Check door operator adjustment (speed, force)
4. Verify photo eye alignment and cleanliness
5. Test safety edge switch
6. Check door interlock contacts

#### Symptom: Rough or Noisy Ride
1. Check guide shoes or roller guides for wear
2. Inspect guide rails for damage or misalignment
3. Check machine alignment
4. Inspect wire ropes for uneven wear
5. Check sheave bearings
6. Verify brake release is complete

#### Symptom: Car Not Leveling Properly
1. Check leveling sensors (vanes or magnets)
2. Inspect motor brake adjustment
3. Verify valve operation (hydraulic elevators)
4. Check for rope stretch (traction elevators)
5. Inspect controller logic and parameters

#### Symptom: Hydraulic Elevator Drifting Down
1. Check lowering valve for leaks (internal bypass)
2. Inspect cylinder packing
3. Check oil temperature (hot oil bypasses more easily)
4. Test check valve operation
5. **NOTE: Persistent drift indicates seal or valve failure requiring immediate repair**

### 5. Safety Requirements

#### Required Signage
- Maximum capacity (pounds and persons) in each car
- Emergency phone number
- Inspection certificate (current, visible)
- "No Smoking" signs
- Freight elevators: maximum load distribution diagram

#### Emergency Procedures
- Entrapment: Use emergency phone, do not force doors
- Power failure: Emergency lighting and phone must operate on backup
- Flooding in pit: Shut down elevator immediately, pump water, inspect before restarting
- Seismic event: All elevators go to nearest floor and open doors (seismic mode)

#### Regulatory Compliance
- Annual Category 1 test required (ASME A17.1)
- 5-year Category 5 full load test required
- Maintain inspection log for minimum 5 years
- Report any accidents or near-misses within 24 hours
- Keep all modifications documented and approved by elevator inspector
