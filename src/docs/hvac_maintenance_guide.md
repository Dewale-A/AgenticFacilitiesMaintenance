# HVAC Maintenance Guide
## Standard Operating Procedures for Facilities Maintenance

### 1. Air Handling Unit (AHU) Preventive Maintenance

#### Monthly Tasks
- Inspect and replace air filters (MERV-13 or as specified)
- Check belt tension and alignment on belt-driven units
- Inspect drain pans for standing water and algae buildup
- Verify thermostat and control settings

#### Quarterly Tasks
- Lubricate fan bearings (use manufacturer-specified grease)
- Check and tighten electrical connections
- Inspect and clean evaporator and condenser coils
- Test safety controls and limit switches
- Measure and record refrigerant pressures
- Check damper operation and calibration

#### Semi-Annual Tasks
- Full belt replacement on belt-driven units
- Comprehensive coil cleaning (chemical wash if needed)
- Inspect and test economizer operation
- Verify proper airflow with anemometer readings
- Check VFD (Variable Frequency Drive) operation and parameters

#### Annual Tasks
- Complete system performance evaluation
- Vibration analysis on all rotating components
- Electrical insulation testing on motors
- Comprehensive refrigerant leak check
- Ductwork inspection for leaks and damage
- Update preventive maintenance records in CMMS

### 2. Boiler Maintenance Procedures

#### Pre-Season Startup Checklist
- Inspect fireside and waterside of heat exchanger
- Check and clean burner assembly
- Verify all safety controls (pressure relief, low water cutoff)
- Test flame safeguard control sequence
- Check combustion efficiency (CO2, O2, stack temperature)
- Inspect and test all valves (safety relief, isolation, drain)
- Verify water treatment program is active

#### Monthly During Operation
- Check water chemistry (pH, hardness, dissolved solids)
- Blow down low water cutoff to verify operation
- Inspect for leaks at all connections
- Record operating temperatures and pressures
- Check combustion air supply is unobstructed

#### Annual Inspection
- Hydrostatic pressure test
- Full fireside cleaning
- Replace gaskets and seals as needed
- Calibrate all controls and sensors
- Update inspection records for regulatory compliance

### 3. Chiller Maintenance

#### Weekly Checks
- Record operating log (temperatures, pressures, amperage)
- Check oil level and condition
- Inspect for unusual vibration or noise
- Verify condenser water flow and temperature

#### Monthly Tasks
- Check refrigerant charge and record pressures
- Inspect and clean condenser tubes (if water-cooled)
- Check oil filter differential pressure
- Test safety controls

#### Annual Tasks
- Eddy current testing on condenser and evaporator tubes
- Oil analysis
- Refrigerant analysis
- Comprehensive electrical testing
- Vibration analysis
- Update maintenance records in CMMS

### 4. Rooftop Unit (RTU) Maintenance

#### Quarterly Tasks
- Replace air filters
- Clean condenser coils
- Check refrigerant charge
- Inspect and clean drain lines
- Test economizer operation
- Check thermostat calibration

#### Annual Tasks
- Full electrical inspection
- Belt replacement
- Bearing lubrication
- Comprehensive performance test
- Inspect roof curb and flashing for leaks

### 5. Troubleshooting Common HVAC Issues

#### Symptom: Insufficient Cooling
1. Check thermostat settings and operation
2. Inspect air filters (dirty filters reduce airflow by up to 50%)
3. Verify refrigerant charge
4. Check evaporator and condenser coils for fouling
5. Inspect ductwork for leaks or disconnections
6. Verify fan operation and airflow direction

#### Symptom: Unusual Vibration
1. Check belt tension and alignment
2. Inspect bearings for wear (listen for grinding)
3. Verify fan wheel balance
4. Check motor mounts and isolation
5. If vibration persists, perform vibration analysis
6. **NOTE: Recurring vibration issues may indicate end-of-life for the unit**

#### Symptom: High Energy Consumption
1. Check for refrigerant undercharge or overcharge
2. Inspect economizer operation
3. Verify VFD is operating correctly
4. Check for simultaneous heating and cooling
5. Inspect building envelope for air leaks
6. Review scheduling and setpoint optimization

### 6. Emergency Procedures

#### Refrigerant Leak
- Evacuate area if leak is in occupied space
- Ventilate the area
- Do not use open flames or spark-producing equipment
- Contact certified refrigerant technician
- Document leak location and estimated quantity
- Report to environmental compliance officer

#### Boiler Emergency Shutdown
- Activate emergency stop
- Close fuel supply valve
- Do not open boiler until cooled
- Contact boiler technician and facilities supervisor
- Document incident in CMMS
- File incident report within 24 hours

#### Electrical Fire in HVAC Equipment
- Activate fire alarm if not already triggered
- Use Class C fire extinguisher only
- Do not use water on electrical fires
- Shut off power at breaker panel if safe to do so
- Evacuate and wait for fire department
- Document and report per fire safety protocol
